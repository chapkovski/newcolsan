The Task

Each participant is randomly assigned to a group of three. There are two groups: A and B, with three participants in each group.

We call a participant from the group A: Participant A, and a participant from the group B: Participant B.

You have been assigned to the group {{ player.subgroup }}. Your decisions will affect your own outcome, the outcomes of the other members of your group ({{ player.subgroup }}), and the outcomes of the members of another group ({{ player.another_subgroup_name }}). Likewise, the decisions of the other members of your group and of the other group will affect your outcome.

The experiment consists of {{ Constants.num_rounds }} rounds. In each round you have to take the following decision:

Decision:

You are given {{ Constants.endowment }} points and you have to decide how much, if any, to transfer to the other participant. The amount of money you decide to transfer will be multiplied by {{ Constants.pd_factor }} and earned by the other participant. The other participant will be given the same choice.

For instance:

  • If you both transfer everything, you both get {{ max_pd }} points and are better off than you you both keep everything.
  • If you transfer everything and the other person keeps everything, then you earn nothing in this stage.
  • If you keep everything and the other participant transfers everything, then you get {{ egoistic_pd }} points and your partner will earn nothing.

Your partner from group {{ player.another_subgroup_name }} has the same incentives as you. You and your partner will make your decisions simultaneously.
