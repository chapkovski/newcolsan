The Task

Each participant is randomly assigned to a group of three. There are two groups: A and B, with three participants in each group.

We call a participant from the group A: Participant A, and a participant from the group B: Participant B.

You have been assigned to a group A. Your decisions will affect your own outcome, the outcomes of the other members of your group (A), and the outcomes of the members of another group (B). Likewise, the decisions of the other members of your group and of the other group will affect your outcome.

The experiment consists of {{Constants.num_rounds}} rounds. Each round consists of two stages as follows:

Stage 1:

At the beginning of Stage 1 you will be matched with a random participant from a group B. Each other member of your group A will also be matched randomly with another participant of a group B.

You and your partner must choose between two actions: RED or BLUE. Your choice affects your earnings and the earnings of your partner.

  • If you both choose RED: you gain {{Constants.yesyes_payoff}} points, and your partner gains {{Constants.yesyes_payoff}} points.
  • If you choose RED and your partner chooses BLUE: you gain {{Constants.yesno_payoff}}, your partner gains {{Constants.noyes_payoff}}.
  • If you choose BLUE and your partner chooses RED: you gain {{Constants.noyes_payoff}}, your partner gains {{Constants.yesno_payoff}}.
  • If you both choose BLUE: you gain {{Constants.nono_payoff}}, your partner gains {{Constants.nono_payoff}}.

These choices are depicted in the table below. The first entry in each cell represents your payoff for that round, while the second entry represents the payoff of the person you are matched with.
